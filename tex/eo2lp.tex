\begin{abstract}
	\noindent
	Eunoia is an emerging logical framework designed for
	specifying the proofs and proof systems of SMT solvers,
	namely \texttt{cvc5}.
	%
	We present a translation from Eunoia to the
	\emph{$λΠ$-calculus modulo rewriting} as implemented
	by the $\lambdapi$ proof assistant.
	%
	The translation is implemented by our tool \texttt{eo2lp},
	which we use for generating $\lambdapi$ encodings of
	%
	(a)~a large fragment of \texttt{cvc5}'s
	\textit{Cooperating Proof Calculus} (CPC), and
	%
	(b)~proofs produced by \texttt{cvc5} on \texttt{unsat}
	problems from various fragments of SMT-LIB.
\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion and Future Work}\label{sec:conclu}

We have presented a translation from Eunoia specifications and proofs
to the $\lambda\Pi$-calculus, as implemented in the $\lambdapi$ proof
assistant. Our tool \texttt{eo2lp} automates this translation,
and we have demonstrated it on CPC-MINI and on proof scripts
from a range of SMT-LIB fragments.
% TODO: update with final benchmark numbers.

For future work, covering the full CPC signature would enable
translation of arbitrary \cvc{5} proofs. The remaining encoding
failures are concentrated in theories not yet included in CPC-MINI
(nonlinear arithmetic, strings, bitvectors), which require extending
both the signature and the elaboration logic.
%
Another direction is proof interoperability: the $\lambdapi$
encodings can be exported to other proof assistants (e.g.\ Rocq,
Isabelle/HOL) via the Dedukti ecosystem, enabling independent
verification of SMT solver results.
