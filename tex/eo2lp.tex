\begin{abstract}
	\noindent
	Eunoia is an emerging logical framework for
	specifying the proofs and proof systems of \cvc{5}.
	%
	We present a translation from a core fragment of Eunoia
	to the \emph{$λΠ$-calculus modulo rewriting} as implemented
	by the LambdaPi proof assistant.
	%
	The translation is implemented by our tool \texttt{eo2lp},
	which we use for generating LambdaPi encodings of
	%
	(a)~a large fragment of the
	\textit{Cooperating Proof Calculus} (CPC),
	the Eunoia signature defining \cvc{5}'s proof system, and
	%
	(b)~proofs produced by \cvc{5} on \texttt{unsat}
	problems from various fragments of SMT-LIB.
\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion and Future Work}\label{sec:conclu}

We have presented a translation from Eunoia to the
$\lambda\Pi$-calculus modulo rewriting that encodes
\cvc{5}'s proof system---inference rules,
side-condition programs, and elaboration semantics---automatically,
unlike approaches that hand-write soundness lemmas for
each rule~\cite{coltellacciReconstructionSMTProofs2024a,mohamed25leansmt}.
%
The trade-off is that the translated rules are taken
as axioms rather than proved sound; their
well-typedness and subject reduction are nevertheless
verified by LambdaPi.
%
Our tool \texttt{eo2lp} translates all 22~modules of
CPC-MINI and independently verifies 97\% of
1{,}036~proof scripts across 14~SMT-LIB fragments.

The most direct future work is extending CPC-MINI
to the full CPC (bitvectors, strings, datatypes, reals)
and undertaking a thorough comparison with
Ethos---coverage, performance, error detection, and
the kinds of upstream bugs each catches.
%
Because the encoding lives in LambdaPi, it also serves
as a basis for proving individual rules sound
directly, or---via the Dedukti ecosystem---for
exporting the CPC representation to other proof
assistants such as Rocq or Isabelle/HOL, analogously
to IsaRare~\cite{lachnitt24isarare}.
%
More speculatively, the typed interpretation
developed in \autoref{sec:lambdapi} is a step toward
a formal semantics for Eunoia in the absence of an
official one, and a foundation on which to study
the higher-order extensions proposed for
SMT-LIB~3~\cite{barrettSMTLIB3Bringing,smt3-proposal}.
