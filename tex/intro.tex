\section{Background and Motivation}\label{sec:intro}
%
The area of automated reasoning known as
\emph{satisfiability modulo theories} (SMT)
focuses on the development of tools for
checking the satisfiability of first-order
formulas over combinations of mathematical
theories~\cite{Barrett2021}.
%
Such tools (known as SMT \emph{solvers}) are
increasingly used as back-ends for various tasks:
hardware~\cite{DBLP:conf/fmcad/MattareiMBDHH18} and
software verification~\cite{barnett06boogie,riant23debugging,kosmatov16frama-c},
%
model checking \cite{DBLP:conf/cav/ChampionMST16},
cyber-security \cite{Backes2018},
and to improve automation in proof
assistants~\cite{DBLP:conf/cpp/ArmandFGKTW11,DBLP:journals/jar/BlanchetteBP13}.
%
Because these applications demand a high level
of confidence in the results produced by the solver,
many solvers are capable of generating
\emph{proof certificates}
that demonstrate the absurdity of specifications
deemed unsatisfiable by the solver.
%

Towards the aim of standardizing and benchmarking solvers,
the \emph{SMT library initiative} oversees the development of
the \emph{SMT-LIB standard} \cite{Barrett2015-standard},
which specifies the syntax and semantics of a
common language used for interacting with solvers.
%%
Proof generation is however not covered by the standard,
which has led to the development of various proof formats,
namely LFSC, Alethe, and recently Eunoia.
%
Eunoia is a proof output language and a logical framework:
it allows formalizing the constants, side-condition programs
and inference rules used by an SMT solver.
%
In particular, the proof system of \cvc{5} is
formalized as a Eunoia signature,
allowing its proofs to be checked by the C++ tool
Ethos~\cite{EthosUser_manualmdMain}.
%

The $\lambda\Pi$-calculus modulo rewriting~\cite{CousineauDowek}
and its implementations in the tools Dedukti~\cite{assaf2023dedukti}
and LambdaPi~\cite{hondet:hal-02981561} are designed to
offer a trustworthy proof checker with an emphasis on
interoperability of proof systems.
%
This paper presents a translation from Eunoia to LambdaPi
and an OCaml tool $\texttt{eo2lp}$ that implements the
translation.
%
We used our tool to translate a large portion of
\cvc{5}'s proof system to LambdaPi (\autoref{fig:pipeline}),
allowing us to further translate and typecheck
thousands of proof scripts produced by running \cvc{5}
on $\texttt{unsat}$ problems from the SMT-LIB fragments for logic,
linear integer arithmetic, arrays, and sets.

The most closely related work is that of
Coltellacci et
al.~\cite{coltellacciReconstructionSMTProofs2024a},
who developed a translation of
\emph{Alethe}~\cite{schurrAletheGenericSMT2021}
proofs into $\lambdapi$.
%
Alethe draws from a fixed set of inference rules
designed to reflect the reasoning mechanisms of SMT
solvers; its proofs are produced by
\textsc{veriT}~\cite{DBLP:conf/cade/BoutonODF09} and
\cvc{5}~\cite{Barbosa2022}, and can be checked by
Carcara~\cite{andreottiCarcaraEfficientProof2023}.
%
The common interface provided by Alethe has enabled
interoperability with proof
assistants~\cite{DBLP:conf/cpp/ArmandFGKTW11,DBLP:conf/cade/SchurrFD21}.
%
Coltellacci et al.\ extend Carcara with a $\lambdapi$
back-end in which each Alethe inference rule corresponds
to a fixed lemma, and proofs are reconstructed by
composing these lemmas.
%
By contrast, our translation targets Eunoia, so
our tool translates both the proof system
and individual proof scripts; it is then the
responsibility of the signature developer to ensure
the soundness of the inference rules used.

The idea of reducing proof checking for SMT to type
checking originates with
LFSC~\cite{DBLP:journals/fmsd/StumpORHT13}, which
extends the Edinburgh Logical Framework (LF) with side
conditions---programs that restrict applications of
inference rules---and our translation preserves this
aspect by encoding side-condition programs as
$\lambdapi$ rewrite rules.
%
LFSC was introduced for \cvc{3} and its successors,
but several proof rules of \cvc{5} are not supported by
this back-end; such steps are given as axioms and must be
trusted.


Eunoia also shares many aspects of the speculative
SMT-LIB~3 proposal~\cite{barrettSMTLIB3Bringing},
which explores extending SMT-LIB with dependent
types and parametric polymorphism,
and proof-specific constructs such as inference
rules and side-conditions~\cite{smt3-proposal}.
%
Brown~\cite{Brown2021} observes that SMT-LIB~3's
expressive type system---situated between simple type
theory and the Calculus of Inductive
Constructions---naturally calls for proof
representations in type-theoretic frameworks, and
proposes CIC proof terms and proof terms for a
set theory axiomatized in simple type theory
as two candidate proof frameworks.
%
Our work combines aspects of both approaches:
%
Eunoia types are identified with $\lambdapi$ terms
of type $\Set$, and proofs terms use the dependent
types and rewriting native to the $λΠ$-calculus.

Proofs from a wide range of systems have been embedded
into Dedukti:
proof assistants such as
Rocq~\cite{boespflug12coqine},
Isabelle~\cite{isabelle_dedukti},
HOL~Light~\cite{assaf15translating},
Matita~\cite{krajono},
and Lean~\cite{vaishnav25lean4less};
automated theorem provers such as
Zenon~Modulo, iProverModulo~\cite{burel20first},
ArchSAT~\cite{bury:tel-02612985},
Vampire~\cite{komel2025case},
and more generally any prover outputting proofs in
TPTP format~\cite{DBLP:conf/flairs/SutcliffeBB25};
and even the programming language semantic framework
$\mathbb{K}$~\cite{DBLP:conf/types/LedeinBD22}.
%
Conversely, proofs in Dedukti can be exported to
Rocq, Matita, or
HOL/Light~\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/abs-1807-01873}.
%
In the next section, we present Eunoia and CPC.
\Autoref{sec:lambdapi} defines the $\lambda\Pi$-calculus
modulo rewriting and gives the translation.
Results are presented in \autoref{sec:results}, and we
conclude in \autoref{sec:conclu}.
